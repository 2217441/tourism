Popular Destinations in Malaysia

Here are some of the most popular destinations that our customers have enjoyed visiting in Malaysia. You can click on each destination to learn more about it and see some photos.

Kuala Lumpur
The capital city of Malaysia, Kuala Lumpur is a vibrant and modern metropolis with skyscrapers, shopping malls, museums, parks, and more.
Penang
An island state in the northwest of Malaysia, Penang is known for its rich cultural heritage, diverse cuisine, street art, and beaches.
Langkawi
A group of 99 islands in the Andaman Sea, Langkawi is a popular destination for nature lovers, with rainforests, waterfalls, wildlife, and marine life.
Malacca
A historic city in the southwest of Malaysia, Malacca is famous for its colonial architecture, multicultural influences, and local delicacies.
Sabah
A state in the northeast of Borneo island, Sabah is home to Mount Kinabalu, the highest peak in Southeast Asia, as well as diverse ecosystems and wildlife.
Sarawak
A state in the northwest of Borneo island, Sarawak is known for its diverse ethnic groups, cultural festivals, caves, national parks, and wildlife sanctuaries.
Perhentian Islands
A pair of islands in the east coast of Malaysia, Perhentian Islands are a paradise for snorkeling, diving, kayaking, and relaxing on the white sandy beaches.
Cameron Highlands
A highland region in the center of Malaysia, Cameron Highlands are famous for their tea plantations, strawberry farms, flower gardens, and cool climate.
Tioman Island
An island in the South China Sea, Tioman Island is a popular destination for scuba diving, surfing, hiking, and enjoying the tropical scenery.

How to Plan Your Trip to Malaysia

By Your Name on Jan 15, 2022

Malaysia is a beautiful and diverse country that offers something for everyone. Whether you are looking for culture, nature, adventure, or relaxation, you will find it here. However, planning a trip to Malaysia can be overwhelming if you don't know where to start. Here are some tips to help you plan your trip to Malaysia.

  1. Decide when to go. Malaysia has a tropical climate with hot and humid weather all year round. However, there are some variations depending on the region and season. The west coast of Peninsular Malaysia is generally dry from November to February and wet from May to October. The east coast of Peninsular Malaysia and the islands of Sabah and Sarawak are generally dry from March to October and wet from November to February. The best time to visit Malaysia depends on your preferences and activities.
  2. Decide where to go. Malaysia has a lot of attractions and destinations to choose from. You can visit the bustling capital city of Kuala Lumpur, the historic city of Malacca, the cultural island of Penang, the scenic highlands of Cameron or Genting, the tropical islands of Langkawi or Tioman, the wildlife-rich states of Sabah or Sarawak, and more. You can also combine different regions and islands to get a taste of everything. However, you should also consider your budget, time, and interests when deciding where to go.
  3. Book your flights and accommodation. Once you have decided when and where to go, you can start booking your flights and accommodation. You can use online platforms such as Skyscanner or Expedia to compare prices and options for flights and hotels. You can also use Airbnb or Booking.com to find alternative accommodation such as apartments or homestays. You should book your flights and accommodation as early as possible to get the best deals and availability.
  4. Plan your itinerary and activities. After booking your flights and accommodation, you can start planning your itinerary and activities. You can use online resources such as TripAdvisor or Lonely Planet to find out what to see and do in each destination. You can also use Google Maps or Maps.me to plan your routes and transportation. You should also check the weather forecast and local events before you go.
  5. Pack your essentials and enjoy your trip. Finally, you can pack your essentials and get ready for your trip. You should pack light and bring only what you need. Some of the essentials you should bring are your passport, visa, flight tickets, accommodation confirmation, travel insurance, money, credit cards, phone, charger, adapter, camera, sunscreen, insect repellent, hat, sunglasses, umbrella, raincoat, clothes, shoes, toiletries, medications, and a copy of your itinerary. You should also check the customs and immigration regulations of Malaysia before you go. Once you have everything ready, you can enjoy your trip to Malaysia and have a wonderful time.
